The Vision

One Platform. Every Event. Every Teammate.

DW SciOly Hub replaces scattered study materials, disconnected practice tests, and guesswork with a single intelligent system that knows each student's strengths, adapts to their gaps, and connects partners so they study smarter — not just harder.

AI

AI-Powered Diagnostics

Upload any practice test and its answer key. The system maps every question to the 2026 Div B rules topics, identifies exactly which sub-areas a student missed, and builds a personalized remediation plan — from general knowledge through advanced mastery.

⚡

Adaptive Learning Paths

A four-stage learning engine — Generalize → Specialize → Refine → Master — adjusts in real-time as students take quizzes and upload new test results. It always targets the highest-impact knowledge gaps first.

🤝

Partner Synergy Engine

For every two-person event, the system maps both partners' strengths and weaknesses side by side. It recommends which topics each partner should own and where they should collaborate to maximize their combined score.

📊

Coach Command Center

Coaches see real-time dashboards for every student in their event. They can assign study topics, schedule quizzes, view progress trends, and identify at-risk students before it's too late.

User Roles

Three Roles, One Connected System

Every user logs in with Google (Gmail), and the system routes them to the right experience. Coach Celeste (Coaches Solesky & Whittern) serve as administrators who can create coach accounts and oversee the whole team.

Student / Olympian

~20 competitors & alternates

  • Personal dashboard with stats across all assigned events
  • Upload practice tests + keys for AI analysis
  • Follow personalized study paths per event
  • Take adaptive quizzes (like Scioly.org, but targeted)
  • View partner's coverage map and shared gap analysis
  • Track mastery progress and improvement streaks
  • Access AI-generated study summaries and mnemonics

Event Coach

  • Dashboard for each event they coach
  • Upload practice tests and assign to specific students
  • View per-student mastery breakdown by sub-topic
  • Create custom quizzes or approve AI-generated ones
  • Flag priority topics for the team to focus on
  • Send study reminders and announcements
  • View partner compatibility and re-assign if needed

Head Coach / Admin

Coaches Solesky & Whittern

  • Full team-wide analytics across all 23 events
  • Create and manage coach accounts
  • Assign students to events and manage pairings
  • View competition readiness scores by event
  • Track attendance and engagement metrics
  • Manage competition schedule and conflict blocks
  • Export reports for team meetings and planning

Pedagogy

The Four-Stage Learning Engine

Inspired by your brainstorm: "first you generalize, then specialize, then refine." We've added a fourth mastery stage to push students toward nationals-level depth. The AI engine determines which stage each student is in per event and per sub-topic.

1

Generalize

Build baseline knowledge across all sub-topics in the event. Ensure students "know their RNA from their DNA." Broad quizzes cover the full rules scope.

2

Specialize

AI identifies weak areas from test uploads. Students receive targeted study materials, summaries, and focused quizzes on specific sub-topics they missed.

3

Refine

Move into State-level and Nationals-level depth. Cover advanced topics, edge cases, and connections between sub-areas. Cross-reference partner gaps.

4

Master

Simulate competition conditions. Timed practice tests, past state/nationals question patterns, and continuous review of previously mastered content to prevent decay.

📤

Test Upload & Analysis

Students or coaches upload a practice test image/PDF plus its answer key. The AI engine matches each question to the 2026 Div B rules topics (e.g., "Anatomy → Nervous System → Cranial Nerves" or "Rocks & Minerals → Hardness Scale"). It produces a topic-level heatmap showing strengths and gaps. Year-specific topics are treated as distinct entities — "Anatomy: Nervous System 2026" is separate from prior years' focus areas.

🧠

Adaptive Quiz Engine

Inspired by the Scioly.org question model. Short, focused questions drawn from a bank aligned to the rules manual. Questions adapt based on the student's current stage: broad for Stage 1, hyper-specific for Stages 3–4. Includes personalized mnemonics, study summaries, and source links for wrong answers. Spaced repetition keeps mastered topics from decaying.

📊

Progress Tracking & Mastery

Mastery is measured by improvement rate across consecutive tests and quizzes — not just a single score. A topic is "mastered" when a student demonstrates consistent accuracy across multiple assessments. Even mastered topics receive periodic review questions. If accuracy drops, the system automatically re-queues study materials for that area.

Competitive Edge

Partner Synergy System

Most Div B events are two-person teams. The difference between 1st and 5th place is often how well partners complement each other. This system makes that visible and actionable.

Coverage Map

A visual heatmap comparing both partners' mastery across every sub-topic in an event. Green = both strong. Yellow = one partner covers it. Red = gap for both. The goal: eliminate all red zones before State.

Smart Assignments

The AI suggests which partner should "own" each sub-topic based on current mastery, learning velocity, and available study time. Partners study different areas to maximize combined knowledge coverage.

Joint Study Sessions

Partners can quiz each other through the platform. The system generates questions where one partner teaches the other, reinforcing learning. Coaches can see which pairs are actively collaborating.

Key User Flows

How It Works in Practice

1

Test Upload Flow

Student/Coach uploads a practice test (photo, PDF, or typed) plus the answer key. They select which event it's for from a dropdown of all 23 events.

AI processes: Maps each question to 2026 rules topics → Compares student answers to key → Generates a topic-level heatmap → Identifies top 3 priority gaps → Creates a study assignment with recommended resources and a 5-question mini-quiz to start.

2

Daily Study Flow

Student opens the app and sees their "Today's Focus" card: one event, one sub-topic, with a 5-minute reading summary (AI-generated from reliable sources) plus 10 adaptive questions.

After the quiz: Wrong answers get immediate explanations + mnemonics. The system schedules a review of missed topics in 1, 3, and 7 days (spaced repetition). The student's mastery score updates in real-time for both them and their coach.

3

Partner Check-in Flow

Partner view shows a side-by-side mastery grid. Green cells = both partners know it. Gold cells = only one partner knows it. Red cells = neither partner knows it.

The system suggests: "Alex, focus on Cranial Nerves (your partner has it). Jordan, focus on Endocrine Disorders (Alex has it covered). Both of you need to review Spinal Cord Tracts together." Partners can start a joint quiz session from this view.

4

Coach Review Flow

Coach opens their event page and sees a team readiness score (average mastery), a ranked list of students by improvement rate, and flagged risk areas where no student is above 70%.

Coach actions: Assign specific topics for the week → Upload a custom practice test → Schedule a timed mock test → Send motivational messages → Recommend student pairings based on complementary strengths.

Rollout Plan

From Concept to Competition

A phased approach to get the platform live before State, with incremental features building toward Nationals readiness.

Phase 1 — Week 1–2 (Immediate)

Foundation & MVP

Set up Next.js project, Supabase database, Google OAuth login. Build the student dashboard skeleton, event sidebar navigation, and test upload flow. Load the 2026 Div B rules manual topics into the database as the master topic tree. Deploy to Vercel. Goal: students can log in, see their events, and upload a test.

Phase 2 — Week 2–3

AI Analysis Engine

Integrate Claude API for test analysis. Build the pipeline: test upload → AI topic mapping → gap identification → study path generation. Create the mastery heatmap visualization. Build the first version of the adaptive quiz engine with a starter question bank for the 8 study events.

Phase 3 — Week 3–4 (Pre-State Launch)

Partner System & Coach Dashboard

Build the partner synergy view with side-by-side mastery comparison. Add the coach dashboard with per-student progress tracking. Enable coach-assigned quizzes and study topics. Add the competition countdown and schedule view. Launch to the full team of ~20 students + coaches before State.

Phase 4 — Post-State → Pre-Nationals

Advanced Features

Add timed mock test mode for competition simulation. Expand question bank with past state/nationals patterns. Build the spaced repetition engine for long-term retention. Add lab/build event features (design logs, procedure guides). Polish the mobile PWA experience for on-the-go studying.

Phase 5 — Nationals Prep

Competition Mode

Nationals-specific topic depth unlocked. Conflict block schedule optimizer. Event-by-event readiness dashboard for head coaches. Final push: identify remaining red zones across all 23 events and generate targeted study plans for the last 2 weeks.

Winning Strategy

Why This Platform Wins Championships

Daniel Wright has won State 14 consecutive years. This tool is designed to maintain that dominance and convert it into a National title by addressing the specific challenges of competing at the highest level.

Eliminate Blind Spots

At nationals, every team is strong. The difference is who has fewer gaps. The AI analysis across multiple test uploads reveals sub-topics that students think they know but consistently get wrong. These hidden weaknesses are where championships are lost.

Maximize Partner Efficiency

Two students studying the same topics is wasted effort. The partner synergy system ensures every pair covers the maximum possible breadth and depth. When one partner masters a topic, the other moves to the next gap — automatically.

State vs. Nationals Depth

The rules manual explicitly differentiates content by level. The platform tracks this: topics are tagged as "All Levels," "State & National Level Only," or "National Level Only." Students progressively unlock deeper content as they master the basics.

Year-Specific Topic Isolation

As you noted: "Anatomy for the endocrine system" is a separate entity from "Anatomy for the nervous system." The platform treats each year's event focus as its own knowledge domain, preventing confusion from prior years' content.

Coach Visibility

With 30+ parent volunteer coaches across events, coordination is a challenge. The admin dashboard gives head coaches a bird's-eye view of the entire team's readiness. No student falls through the cracks. No event goes unmonitored.

Continuous Improvement Loop

Every invitational, regional, and state test becomes training data. The more tests uploaded, the smarter the system gets at identifying patterns in what's tested. By nationals, students will have practiced not just the content — but the style of questions they'll face.
